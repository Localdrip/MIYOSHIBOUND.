Miyoshikiku (Konaki no Nusumi-zake)

Miyoshikiku Shuzo / Ryoichiro Umamiya







Pursuing an Original Approach to Sake

In the remote gorge of Oboke, where yokai legends still breathe, the "Konaki no Nusumi-zake" you encounter here overturns traditional notions of sake. The goal was to craft a sake that harmonizes with the increasingly diverse modern dining table. Its sweet-and-tart, fruit-like acidity and floral, fruity aroma pursue a style that is the exact opposite of the classic dry, clean profile. Like a fine white wine, it crosses borders and genres, offering a new possibility to captivate even those unfamiliar with sake.





Original In-house Yeast

The secret to this fruity acidity lies in the yeast. One day, during brewing, they happened upon a batch with an intriguingly different aroma. They isolated and cultivated it, developing it into a completely original in-house yeast found nowhere else. There are no compromises in the raw materials either: the water is local spring water fetched personally from the mountains, and all the sake rice is purchased directly from local farmers whose faces and stories they know. Much like winemaking, they maintain a dialogue with producers to preserve the distinct flavors of the region.





The Spirit of Brewing and Rock

"Stay true to what you want to do." At the heart of Miyoshikiku's brewing lies the punk rock spirit that is also the toji's origin. While deeply respecting the old-fashioned methods that stretch back to the Edo period, they do not simply trace the past. They flexibly adopt new techniques and values to suit modern lifestyles. Rather than walking the rails, guided by the belief to "walk the wild side," Miyoshikiku continues to pursue further evolution in sake, embracing exciting and bold challenges every day.





From the Producer

Ryoichiro Umamiya

Miyoshikiku Shuzo / Miyoshi City, Tokushima Prefecture

We hope this sake will be enjoyed not only as a special pairing for washoku, but also as an everyday companion to regular meals. Free from rules, we want people to discover their own liberating ways to enjoy it alongside the dishes they love.
