The Spirit of "Itadakimasu"

In Iya, Tokushima — where the original landscapes of Japan remain — people live in close harmony with the mountains, weaving their abundant gifts into daily life and fostering a deep reverence for nature. How should the community respond to the overabundance of wild deer? Their answer was to honor those lives, ensuring nothing is wasted and treating them as treasured "mountain bounty" to be fully appreciated. The phrase "itadakimasu" signifies receiving life itself; this profound respect for wild animals as precious life has transformed gibier into a special ingredient that can be enjoyed easily in a single can.





Not Just Consuming — Savoring to Perfection

Because we take life, we aim to deliver it in the most delicious condition possible. That commitment is reflected in uncompromising craftsmanship. We select only venison that has passed rigorous quality control, and perform delicate trimming and precise cooking by hand—tasks machines cannot handle. Each cut is made by reading the grain of the meat, and after many trials we arrived at a familiar, approachable seasoning. Beyond the usual image of gibier as a luxury ingredient, the result is astonishingly tender meat with a rich, full flavor.





The Craftsmanship of Iya's Residents

Enjoy it as a snack with sake, add it to your campfire meals, or use it however you like—the possibilities are endless. This healthy venison can, equally enjoyed across borders and cultures, is the crystallization of artisans' passion to create something truly one-of-a-kind. Within this small can lives the love for Iya's majestic nature and the pride of people who delight in handcraft.
